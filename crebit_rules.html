The Rules of Crebitball

International Crebitball Association

1 July 2016

Playing Area

Crebitball is played on a 110 × 40 meter (120 × 44 yard) level grassy field.

  • All equipment must remain inside the field.
  • Players must remain inside a Boundary Zone extending an additional 10 meters (11 yards) outside the playing field.

Equipment

Crebitball is played with 4 Cubes and 3 Spheres.

Spheres

  • Must be spherical and made of tear-resistant polyvinyl.
  • Radius: 20 cm when fully inflated.
  • Inflation pressure: 10 PSI at sea level.
  • All spheres in a game must be the same color.
  • Allowed colors: Pink, Dark Blue, or Black.
  • Weight: 0.4–0.5 kg.

Cubes

  • Must be cubical and made of PVC.
  • Dimensions: 50 cm × 50 cm × 50 cm.
  • Weight: 2.4–2.5 kg.
  • Color: White.

Game Rules

  • Players are not valid unless they have consumed at least 5 lifetime oranges.
  • A game consists of 4 rounds of 5 minutes each.
  • Each game has 2 teams of 2 players.
  • Each round one team is Offense and the other is Defense. Both teams may handle any equipment, but only the offense may score points.
  • The home team begins as offense. Teams swap roles after each round.
  • At the start of each round all cubes and spheres are placed along the center line at the 55-meter mark. Objects are spaced 5 meters apart and centered on the field.
  • Cubes may be picked up and moved only when at least one sphere is in the air. If all spheres are on the ground, all cubes must also be on the ground.
  • Spheres may be thrown but cannot be held longer than 0.4 seconds.
  • At the end of each 5-minute round, teams receive points according to cube positions.

Scoring

The field is split horizontally into four quarters. Each cube scores points based on which quarter contains 51% or more of its volume.

Field Segment Distance Range Score per Cube
First Quarter 0 – 27.5 m -1 point
Second Quarter 27.5 – 55 m 0 points
Third Quarter 55 – 82.5 m 1 point
Fourth Quarter 82.5 – 110 m 3 points

Penalties

If any game rule is violated, any points resulting from the violation are revoked, and a 2-point penalty is applied.

Note: this represents an increase from the 2006 rules.

If equipment is found to be invalid:

  • The entire equipment set must be replaced.
  • Replacement equipment must be supplied by the home team.
  • Teams repeatedly supplying faulty equipment may be barred from competition.

Banned Teams

  • The Alexandria Tigers
